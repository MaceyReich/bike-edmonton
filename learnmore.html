Maps & Routes

Popular Routes

Expand Map +

Birch Tree Trail

Enjoy a short stroll through the woods behind the John Janzen Nature Centre. This 20 minute loop has a few steep sections.

Expand Map +

Commonweatlh Walkway

The Edmonton's Commonwealth Walkway - is a designated 10 km loop. This initiative aims to connect the Commonwealth with a 1,000 km walkable path, linking 100 cities, permanently marking 2,000 points of significance.

Expand Map +

Heritage Trail

The Heritage Trail is a tree-lined walking route that approximates the original path from the Hudson's Bay Company Fort to the settlement of Edmonton. Plaques and sites of historical importance can be found along the route.

Expand Map +

River Loop Trail

This loop around Fort Edmonton Park follows paved and gravel shared pathways along the bank of the river and the hillside behind the John Janzen Nature Centre.

Laws, Regulations, and Safety

Traffic laws are governed both by municipal bylaws as well as provincial regulations, and vary by location (e.g. St. Albert has different bylaws than Edmonton, and laws that apply while riding in Edmonton’s river valley parks may be different than those that apply when riding on shared-use paths adjacent to roads).

The information provided by Bike Edmonton includes partial excerpts and is provided for convenience only, and does not constitute legal advice. If you have any questions or concerns, please contact us.

Our vision is a city where cycling is safe, widespread, and accessible to people of all comfort levels.

We acknowledge that we are all treaty people and we are committed to equity and justice for all our relations.
